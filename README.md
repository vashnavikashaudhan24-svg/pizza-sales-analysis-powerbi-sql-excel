### 3. üéØ Business Problem
  The pizza store lacked a centralized, data-driven system to track its performance. Management needed a comprehensive report based on historical sales data (Jan 2015 ‚Äì Dec 2015) to        answer critical questions such as:
- Which pizza varieties are underperforming or driving maximum profit?
- When are the peak sales hours and months for optimal staffing and inventory?
- What is the best-selling pizza size and category?

### 4. ü•Ö Goal of Dashboard
  The primary goal was to transform raw sales data into actionable business intelligence. The dashboard aims to provide a single-view, interactive platform enabling stakeholders to:
- Monitor performance through core KPIs instantly.
- Identify high-growth opportunities and areas needing improvement (e.g., promotional campaigns for slow-moving items).
- Make data-backed decisions regarding inventory management and marketing schedules.

### 6. üí° Business Impact and Insights
  The analysis resulted in several significant findings:
- High-Profit Drivers: The Classic Deluxe Pizza is confirmed as the overall top-performing item by both revenue and order volume.
- Targeted Marketing: Sales peak on Fridays and Saturdays and during January/July. Marketing efforts should be prioritized during these periods.
- Inventory Focus: The Classic category and Large size pizzas contribute the most to sales, ensuring adequate stock for these items is critical.
- Actionable Strategy: The Brie Carre Pizza, identified as the lowest revenue generator, requires a clear promotional or price review strategy.
